Peter Jackson

Birth date:

31 October 1961

Birth place:

Pukerua Bay, New Zealand

Biography

Director, producer, screenwriter and actor Sir Peter Robert Jackson film is well known for his The Lord of the Rings film trilogy (2001 to 2003), adapted from the novel by J. R. R. Tolkien. Jackson was born in 1961 on New Zealand.

He won international attention early in his career with his "splatstick" horror comedies such as Bad Taste (1987) and Braindead (1992), before coming to mainstream prominence with Heavenly Creatures (1994), for which he shared an Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay nomination with his wife, Fran Walsh. Jackson has been awarded three Academy Awards in his career, including the award for Best Director in 2003; he also won the BAFTA, Golden Globe and Saturn Award for Best Direction the same year.

His films also include Meet the Feebles (1989), The Frighteners (1996), remake of classic movie King Kong (2005), adaptation of Alice Sebold's bestseller The Lovely Bones (2009), and the upcoming The Lord of the Rings prequels The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey (2012) and The Hobbit: There and Back Again (2013). He is also the producer of box office hit District 9 (2009) and The Adventures of Tintin (2011) directed by Steven Spielberg.

Jackson was made a Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit in 2002. He was later knighted (as a Knight Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit) by Governor-General Anand Satyanand at a ceremony in Wellington in April, 2010.
